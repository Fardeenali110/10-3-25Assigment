JPG (or JPEG) stands for Joint Photographic Experts Group, the team that created this format. It is one of the most commonly used image formats for digital photography and web graphics. JPG images use lossy compression, meaning some image quality is lost to reduce file size. The format supports millions of colors, making it ideal for detailed images and photographs. JPG files usually have the extensions and are widely compatible with devices and software. Due to compression, JPG images have smaller file sizes, making them easy to store and share. They are not ideal for images requiring , like logos or text-based graphics. JPG images do not support transparency, unlike PNG files. They are commonly used in websites, social media, and digital cameras. Editing and saving a JPG multiple times can reduce its quality due to repeated compression.

SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) is a popular image format used for creating vector-based graphics. Unlike JPG or PNG, SVG images are made up of mathematical paths instead of pixels, allowing them to be resized without losing quality. This makes them perfect for logos, icons, and web graphics that need to look sharp on any screen. Since SVG files are XML-based, they can be edited with code, making them customizable and interactive. They also support animation and transparency, adding more flexibility to design. SVG files are usually smaller in size compared to high-resolution raster images, which helps improve website performance. Because they are text-based, search engines can read and index them, making them SEO-friendly. However, SVG is not ideal for complex images like photographs, as it works best with simple shapes and designs. Many modern web technologies, including HTML and CSS, fully support SVG. This format is widely used in web development, graphic design, and UI/UX design.

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is a popular image format known for its high quality and lossless compression. Unlike JPG, PNG preserves image details without reducing quality, making it ideal for logos, icons, and digital art. One of its key features is support for transparency, allowing images with transparent backgrounds to blend seamlessly into designs. PNG files also support millions of colors, making them great for detailed graphics. However, due to lossless compression, PNG file sizes are usually larger than JPG files. This format is widely used in web design, image editing, and UI/UX development. Since PNG maintains sharp edges and fine details, it is perfect for text-based graphics and illustrations. Unlike SVG, PNG is a raster image format, meaning it is made of pixels and can lose quality when resized. PNG files are compatible with almost all devices and browsers, making them a reliable choice for high-quality graphics.
